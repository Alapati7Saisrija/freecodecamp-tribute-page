Nara Chandrababu Naidu

Visionary Leader

Chandrababu naidu was entitled as a Apara Bhagiradha after launching a pattiseema project

Here's a time line of CBN life:

  • 1950 - Born on April 20 in chitoor District
  • 1972 - Completed B.A. degree from Sri Venkateswara Arts College, Tirupati.
  • 1974 - Completed Masters in Economics from Sri Venkateswara University
  • 1975 - Joined in Indian Youth Congress and became the president for its local chapter in Pulicherla
  • 1978 - Became an MLA for the Chandragiri constituency at the age of 28
  • Appointed as the minister for cinematography in T. Anjaiah's government.
  • 1980 - Married Bhuvaneswari, N.T RamaRao's second daughter.
  • 1983 - Joined in Telugu Desam Party.
  • 1984 - Demonstrated his political skills when N. Bhaskara Rao staged a coup against N. T. Rama Rao.
  • Ramarao made Naidu in-charge of Karshaka Parishad, the farmers' council established by his government. Ramaraao later appointed Naidu as the general secretary of the party.
  • 1989 - Contested from Kuppam constituency as a TDP candidate and won by 5,000 votes
  • 1994 - Won the elections from Kuppam constituency. He became the Finance and Revenue Minister in N. T. Rama Rao's ministry.
  • 1995 - Came to power as the Chief Minister on September 1.
  • 1996 - Launched the Janmabhoomi (birthplace) Programme in February.
  • 1997 - The 'Clean and Green Campaign' was initiated by CBN.
  • His government started the "self help groups" (SHG) such as water user associations, Vanasamrakshana Samities, Rythu Mithra Groups, CMEY Groups, School Education Committees, and promoted the 'Development of Women and Children in Rural Areas' (DWCRA)
  • 1998 - Atal Bihari Vajpayee, then prime minister of India and Chandrababu Naidu inaugurated the HITEC City (nickanamed Cyberabad) by opening the Cyber Towers, a landmark building in Hyderabad.
  • 1999 - Launched the "Deepam" scheme to provide LPG cooking gas connections to rural women, with a budget of ₹100 crore
  • Rythu Bazaar was launched by Naidu on 26 January with the objective to control the skyrocketing prices of fruits and vegetables
  • Produced a vision statement called "Andhra Pradesh Vision 2020" that outlined various goals and objectives to be achieved by 2020
  • Established the Pharma City, an original, state-of-the-art biotechnology park in the country to the north of Hyderabad
  • 2001 - Launched e-Seva centers in 2001 for paperless and speedy delivery of results to applicants
  • The Naidu government declared the creation of the Cyberabad Development Authority (CDA)
  • 2003 - Survived a land mine blast by People's War Group (PWG) near Alipiri tollgate
  • 2004 - Leader of Opposition
  • 2009 - Leader of Opposition
  • 2014 - The Telugu Desam Party (TDP) returned to power in Andhra Pradesh state, winning 102 seats out of 175 seats .Naidu took oath as the first Chief Minister of the residuary state of Andhra Pradesh at Mangalagiri on the grounds of Acharya Nagarjuna University near Guntur.
  • 2015 - NTR Bharosa scheme started in January , aims to provide pension of ₹1,000 per month to more than 40 lakh people who are in their old age, widow, physically challenged, toddy tappers and weavers
  • Announced AMARAVTHI as the new capital of Navyandhrapradesh.The foundation for the city was laid at Uddandarayunipalem on 22 October
  • Naidu inaugurated Godavari–Krishna river interlink in September .
  • Naidu inaugurated Thotapalli irrigation project
  • 2017 - Inaugurated the first phase of Purushothapatnam Lift Irrigation Scheme
  • Inaugurated the Muchumarri Lift irrigation scheme.
  • 2018 - Introduced bike ambulances by Naidu.
  • Offered ₹100 crore to farmers who win a Nobel Prize in natural farming under the Zero Budget Natural Farming (ZBNF).
  • Laid foundation to Godavari–Penna rivers interlinking project at an estimated cost of ₹83,796 crore in five phases
  • Mukhyamantri Yuvanestham which aims to provide financial assistance to unemployed youth, was launched in October
  • 2019 - Pasupu Kumkuma , a welfare scheme aimed to provide financial help to women in self-help groups, is launched in February
  • Leader of Opposition
  • 2020 - At a conference held at IIT Bombay, Naidu called for Vision 2050, for the transformation in the country in the next 30 years, sharing his experiences with Vision 2020 document and the growth of HITEC City.
